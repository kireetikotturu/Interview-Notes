# MERN Batch Engagement Structure (Group-Based Learning Model)

## 🎯 Objective
To improve student interactivity, collaboration, and offline attendance by establishing consistent group-based engagement throughout the MERN program.

---

## 🔹 1. Group Formation
- Each batch will be divided into **groups of 4 students** at the start of the program.
- These groups will remain **constant throughout the entire course (8 months)**.
- Group formation should mix students of varying skill levels to promote peer learning.

---

## 🔹 2. Group Representative (Rotational Leadership)
- Each group will have one **Group Representative (GR)**, responsible for weekly coordination and communication with instructors.
- The **GR role rotates every week** — ensuring all members experience leadership, communication, and responsibility.

### Responsibilities of the GR:
- Ensure group attendance (especially offline).
- Collect and share group feedback or blockers during the weekly instructor discussion.
- Update group progress in shared logs or portal (if applicable).

---

## 🔹 3. Weekly Instructor–Group Discussion
- Each week, the instructor will have a **10-minute focused discussion** with **one group**.

### Discussion Points:
- Progress on current module / assignments.
- Doubts or blockers faced by the group.
- Peer feedback within the group.
- Suggestions for improving learning flow.

✅ **Outcome:** Each group gets personalized instructor time every few weeks while staying accountable weekly through GR updates.

---

## 🔹 4. Hackathons (Group-Based)
Conduct **3 hackathons** during the MERN program, each aligned with a key milestone:

| Stage | Timing | Focus | Team |
|--------|---------|--------|-------|
| Hackathon 1 | After DOM Module | JavaScript & DOM manipulation | Same group |
| Hackathon 2 | After React Module | Frontend component building, state handling | Same group |
| Hackathon 3 | After Node Module | Backend + Full-stack integration | Same group |

✅ **Purpose:** Strengthen teamwork, apply learned concepts practically, and encourage healthy competition among groups.

---

## 🔹 5. Group of the Month Recognition
Every month, the **best-performing group** will be recognized as **“Group of the Month”** based on these parameters:

| Criteria | Weightage | Description |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| Offline Attendance | 40% | Consistency and presence at the center |
| Module Questions | 30% | Completion & accuracy of post-class questions |
| Exploration & Initiative | 30% | Efforts in discovering or presenting new topics (self-learning beyond syllabus) |

### Recognition Options:
- Certificate or digital badge
- Photo on “Wall of Fame” / portal
- Small reward (swag, goodies, or early access to next mock interviews)

✅ **Outcome:** Creates friendly competition and continuous motivation to attend offline sessions.

---

## 🔹 6. Group Repository Setup
- Each group will maintain an **independent branch** under a **single GitHub repository** for the batch.

**Example:**
```
main
├── group1/
├── group2/
├── group3/
└── group4/
```

Each branch will contain that group’s code, documentation, and hackathon projects.

### Usage Ideas (for productivity):
- **Code Reviews:** Instructors can comment or review commits directly on GitHub.
- **Learning Tracker:** Each group adds a `README.md` file summarizing weekly learnings or blockers.
- **Showcase Work:** Final hackathon submissions and project snapshots are pushed here.
- **Peer Learning:** Other groups can view and learn from different branches.

✅ **Outcome:** Promotes real-world Git workflow experience + structured collaboration.
